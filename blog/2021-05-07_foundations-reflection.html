The end of the faundation

When looking back a month ago, I couldn't read JavaScript at all. I had a big fear of using Terminal, too. What really surprised me is that I'm not who I was a month ago. I didn't need a year to transform myself into something new. I've decided to leave my reflection on the foundation so that I can remember my original intention of this challenge.

3 new things I learned about yourself and my ego

  1. I can program! — I've always thought becoming a developer and writing a program are someone else's job. I made a decision to become someone who I never thought would become.It's still a bit surreal that I can program.
  2. Never too late to learn — I've never imagined I would study at school after I turned 40. It was simply because I lived in my limitations.
  3. Grateful to struggle — It may sounds insane, but I learned the most when struggling. I needed the struggles to grow wiser. It was painful to face the struggles, but now the fact that I'm facing the struggles means I've encountered a great opportunity to grow.

As far as my ego goes, it like to label things: better or worse, right or wrong, and success or failure so on. It basically comes from comparisons. I'm aware when it does and I've come to pause what it does when it's doing. I think this is where I am at the moment.
